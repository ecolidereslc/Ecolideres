I am from the south of Ecuador, specifically from a city called Loja. I have always been fond of the environment, which led me to pursue an Engineering degree in Environmental Management. As part of my studies, I took courses related to Geographic Information Systems (GIS), finding a way to contribute to environmental protection through spatial data. Since then, I have immersed myself in various courses and online tutorials to enhance my skills in GIS and learn more about climate change, a pressing issue that has been discussed since the 1970s but continues to be underaddressed.

During my university years, I was part of an environmental club and worked with fellow students, particularly those studying environmental science, to foster a greener university community. In 2018, I joined forces with two other individuals to create "Ecolideres La Cascarilla," a young environmental club in Loja. We invited people of all ages to participate in activities such as reforestation, cycling, trash challenges, river clean-ups, and more. This club has since evolved into a foundation that collaborates with local environmental authorities, governments, academia, and the private sector.

Our most significant achievement is the "No ensucies el camino de María" campaign, which aims to reduce single-use plastic waste during the pilgrimage to the Virgin of El Cisne. Every year, this pilgrimage generates tons of plastic waste that ends up in forests and rivers. I am proud to have been the president of Ecolideres when we launched this campaign in 2019.

With every small achievement, every lesson learned, and every piece of advice received, I feel closer to my ultimate goal. Just like the mountain peak that constantly inspires me, I strive to reach new heights, enjoying the journey along the way.

Horchata Lojana

The maps are part of the Horchata Loja project, a traditional beverage from the province of Ecuador. The first map shows the distribution of plants within a garden in the Gualel parish. The second map is an interactive map that displays the locations of the 72 plant species (Ríos et al.) used in Loja province to prepare Horchata.

Forest Fires in Loja

Research of my final project titled "Factors that influence the probability of occurrence of forest fires in Ecuador." In this research, I used climate variables, created some accessibility variables, and data from FIRMS to forecast the probability of forest fires in Canton Loja, using Maximum Entropy modeling and GIS.

Personal projects

Land Use and Land Cover Classification Map of Loja using Deep Learning (CNN)

This map presents a comprehensive Land Use and Land Cover (LULC) classification of the Canton Loja, generated through advanced deep learning techniques. Developed as part of a tutorial from the ClimateChange.AI Summer School, this classification uses a fine-tuned ResNet-50 Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) model, trained to identify 10 distinct land use categories from Sentinel-2 satellite imagery.

Accessibility to educational centers in the Canton Loja - Ecuador

This study analyzed educational accessibility in Loja Canton, where 3.6% of the population is illiterate and rural families face challenges due to transportation costs and remote educational centers. Using a friction layer created from slope, rivers, roads, and land use, accessibility times to educational centers were classified. Key datasets included a 12.5m ALOS PALSAR DEM, river, road, and land use data from government sources, and educational center locations from the Ministry of Education. Layers were processed to assign cost values, calculate slopes, and classify time intervals (15-120+ minutes).
